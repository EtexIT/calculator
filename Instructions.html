1. Project Assessment Framework

Before starting your cost estimation, assess your project using the Project Assessment Framework score:

Characteristic Low (1 point) Medium (2 points) High (3 points)
Budget < 50K 50K - 500K > 500K
Duration < 3 months 3 - 12 months > 12 months
Team Size < 5 members 5 - 15 members > 15 members
Stakeholders Single BPO area Multiple BPO area's Organization-wide
Strategic Importance Low impact Moderate impact High impact on org. goals
Complexity Simple, well-defined Moderate complexity Highly complex, many variables
Risk Level Low risk Moderate risk High risk
Regulatory Requirements None Some compliance needs Strict regulatory oversight

Score Interpretation:

  • 21-24: Full PMO management (25% risk adjustment)
  • 17-20: Shared management between team and PMO (20% risk adjustment)
  • 13-16: Team-led with regular PMO check-ins (15% risk adjustment)
  • 8-12: Team-led with minimal PMO oversight (10% risk adjustment)

Note:

The total score ranges from 8 (minimum: all characteristics scored as Low) to 24 (maximum: all characteristics scored as High). The score determines both the level of PMO involvement and the risk adjustment percentage applied to the total project cost.

2. Cost Estimation Process

Team Costs

Estimate effort for each role across three phases:

  • Validation Phase:
    • • Project Manager: 2-3 days per stakeholder group
    • • Solution Architecture: 3-5 days for initial assessment
    • • Business Analyst: 5-10 days for requirements gathering
    • • Developer: 2-3 days for technical feasibility
    • • Tester: 1-2 days for test strategy
  • Preparation Phase:
    • • Project Manager: 15-20% of total project duration
    • • Solution Architecture: 10-15 days for detailed design
    • • Business Analyst: 15-20 days for detailed specifications
    • • Developer: 5-10 days for technical design
    • • Tester: 5-8 days for test planning
  • Execution Phase:
    • • Project Manager: 20% of development effort
    • • Solution Architecture: 15% of development effort
    • • Business Analyst: 25% during development
    • • Developer: Full development time + 20% buffer
    • • Tester: 30% of development time

Technology Costs

Include all technical infrastructure and tools:

  • Licenses: Software licenses and subscriptions
  • Infrastructure: Hardware and hosting costs
  • Cloud Services: Recurring cloud platform fees
  • Tools: Development and testing tools

External Costs

Account for all external resources:

  • Vendors: External service providers
  • Consultants: Specialized expertise
  • Training: User and team training costs

3. CAPEX vs OPEX Classification

CAPEX Includes:

  • Team costs during preparation & execution
  • One-time technology costs
  • External costs
  • Hardware and infrastructure investments

OPEX Includes:

  • Team costs during validation phase
  • Recurring technology costs
  • Monthly subscriptions and licenses
  • Ongoing maintenance costs

4. Monthly Cost Projection Calculation

S-Curve Distribution

The monthly cost projection uses an S-curve distribution to model realistic project spending patterns. This approach reflects that project costs typically follow a sigmoid pattern where:

  • Initial months have lower spending (project setup)
  • Middle months have the highest spending rate (peak activity)
  • Final months taper off (project closure)

Calculation Method:

  1. Duration Determination:
    • Team days are converted to months (21 working days per month)
    • Maximum duration is taken between team months and technology duration
  2. Monthly Weight Calculation:
    • Uses sigmoid function: 1 / (1 + e-10(x-0.5))
    • x is the progress ratio (month / total months)
    • -10 and 0.5 are curve shaping parameters
  3. Cost Distribution:
    • Weights are normalized to sum to 100%
    • Total project cost is distributed according to normalized weights

Example:

For a 6-month project with €100,000 total budget:

Month Progress Ratio Weight Cost
Month 1 0.08 10% €10,000
Month 2 0.25 15% €15,000
Month 3 0.42 25% €25,000
Month 4 0.58 25% €25,000
Month 5 0.75 15% €15,000
Month 6 0.92 10% €10,000

Key Benefits:

  • More realistic than linear distribution
  • Accounts for project ramp-up and wind-down periods
  • Helps with cash flow planning
  • Better matches typical resource utilization patterns

5. Tips and Best Practices

  • Always include contingency in team and external costs
  • Consider dependencies between different project phases
  • Account for regional differences in daily rates
  • Review historical data from similar projects when available
  • Document all assumptions made during estimation
  • Consider the impact of project risks on cost estimates
